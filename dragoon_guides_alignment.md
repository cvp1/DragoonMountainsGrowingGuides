# Dragoon Mountains Growing Library — Summaries & Alignment Recommendations

**Source-of-truth documents (set the tone):**

1. `dragoon_pepper_growing_guide.pdf` — soil/field peppers
2. `bato_bucket_guide_v3.pdf` — Bato Bucket hydroponic build

All other guides should be re-tuned to match these two in voice, structure, and technical specificity.

---

## 1 · Tone & style standard (derived from the two anchors)

The two source-of-truth guides share a consistent editorial voice. Any other document that drifts from this should be pulled back in line.

- **Locator in header/footer.** "Dragoon Mountains, Cochise County, Arizona" appears on the cover and repeats in the page header. Every guide should carry this on the cover page, and an appropriate short locator/subtitle in the page header.
- **Numbered chapters/sections with a one-line subtitle.** Each chapter opens with a title, a chapter number, and a one-line descriptor (e.g., "Native Chiltepin · climate advantages · Cochise County pepper heritage").
- **Short prose opening, then tables.** Each chapter opens with one or two paragraphs of context, then delivers the operational content in tables.
- **Tables carry specific, quantitative targets.** °F, ppm, EC in mS/cm, pH, g/gal, %shade, GPH, etc. — avoid vague adjectives.
- **Local-first framing.** Every topic is phrased through a Dragoon/Cochise County lens (caliche, Sulphur Springs Valley, monsoon, mesquite, Bisbee, Willcox feed stores, etc.).
- **Star ratings and ■ / ✓ marks** for suitability, matchedness, difficulty.
- **"★ Local tip"** call-out boxes for Dragoon-specific insight.
- **Closing "sense of place" paragraph.** The pepper guide ends with "ancestral pepper country… this landscape was made for chile." Every guide should end with a short equivalent placing the topic in the local landscape.
- **British spellings ("fertiliser", "flavour", "colour")** are used consistently — keep them.
- **Cross-references should be explicit.** When a topic lives authoritatively in another guide, refer to it by name rather than re-stating it (this is where the library is currently weakest).

## 2 · Per-guide structured outline summaries

### A. `dragoon_pepper_growing_guide.pdf` — *Growing Peppers in the Dragoon Mountains* ⭐ ANCHOR

**Scope:** Soil-based pepper growing for Dragoon conditions, all chile varieties.

1. *Why the Dragoons are perfect for peppers* — climate advantages table (day/night temps, monsoon, diurnal swing, UV), Chiltepin as native heritage crop.
2. *Pepper species & variety guide* — five species overview; full variety reference table (SHU, days, height, Dragoon notes) from Bell through Lemon Drop.
3. *The Rocoto special section* — C. pubescens requirements matched to Dragoon conditions; 6-step growing protocol; ID notes (black seeds, purple flowers).
4. *Site selection & soil preparation* — target parameters (pH 6.0–7.0, OM 3–5%), pre-plant amendment steps, companion planting.
5. *Starting seeds & transplanting* — variety-by-variety indoor start timing, germination targets, hardening-off checklist.
6. *Irrigation & water management* — drip schedule by growth stage; dedicated blossom-drop prevention checklist.
7. *Nutrition & fertilisation* — seasonal NPK programme, calcium/BER management, Mg correction for sandy soils.
8. *Seasonal growing calendar* — month-by-month tasks for 4,500–5,500 ft.
9. *Canopy, pruning & support* — staking at transplant, pinching, shade-cloth thresholds, techniques to raise capsaicin.
10. *Pest & disease management* — aphids, mites, thrips, weevil, cutworms, grasshoppers, bacterial wilt, BER, sun-scald, powdery mildew, javelina.
11. *Harvest, drying & processing* — green vs. red by variety; sun/ristra/oven/dehydrator/smoke methods.
12. *Overwintering peppers* — which varieties are worth it; 7-step protocol.

### B. `bato_bucket_guide_v3.pdf` — *Bato Bucket Hydroponic Setup* ⭐ ANCHOR

**Scope:** End-to-end build guide for a Dragoon-Mountain Bato Bucket pepper system.

1. *Equipment & components* — Cropking buckets, 2×3 vinyl downspout drain, 15-gal ACE tub reservoir, RAINPOINT solar pump, coco/perlite, Masterblend 3-part, tools, purchase links.
2. *Bench construction & assembly* — 2×4×8 bench on 8×8×16 CMU supports, ¼" slope, downspout drilling technique (reverse), high-end cap / low-end elbow return, bucket placement.
3. *Plumbing & power* — RAINPOINT solar panel placement, intake submersion, supply-line tees, 10 min / 8 hr schedule, transplant modification, runoff target 10–20 %.
4. *Water treatment — SE Arizona / Cochise County* — source-water testing, bicarbonate pre-treatment 5-step protocol, ranked water sources, local pH-down/up options (prickly pear, citric, phosphoric, oak ash, baking soda).
5. *Nutrient management — Masterblend pepper programme* — mixing order (MB → Epsom → Cal-N last), per-gallon stage table with Mg rule at fruit sizing (2.4 g/gal Epsom), EC ceilings, drift management, deficiency quick-reference.
6. *Irrigation schedule* — RAINPOINT 3×/day programming, stage table, explicit hot-month limitation and workaround (second hose timer), runoff reading.
7. *Maintenance* — daily / weekly tasks, end-of-crop H2O2 + vinegar flush.
8. *Materials & rough cost breakdown* — 20-bucket 2-row build cost (~$315–490 infra; ~$206–345 consumables) with purchase URLs.

## 4 · Cross-document issues that need a decision

**4.1 The pepper Masterblend Mg rule is inconsistent across guides.**

- Bato Bucket (anchor): 2.4 g/gal Epsom at fruit sizing → *authoritative*.
- Hydroponic Pepper Guide: 2.4 g/gal at fruit sizing → matches anchor. ✓
- Modern Masterblend Guide: table shows 1.5 g/gal through fruiting; the Key Tip later says 2.4 g/gal. Conflict. **Fix the table.**
- Hydroponic Nutrients: shows generic 1.2 g/gal (6 g per 5 gal). No per-stage data. **Add crop-specific note.**

**4.2 Bicarbonate pre-treatment language drifts slightly between guides.**

All guides describe the same 5-step protocol but with slightly different phrasing. Standardise on the Bato Bucket Section 4 wording and replicate verbatim (including the "fizzing stops = bicarbonates neutralised" shorthand) across the other three places it appears.

**4.3 Library navigation is missing.**

None of the 8 guides points readers to the other 7. This is the single most valuable narrative change. Add a consistent "This library contains:" block to the front matter of every guide. Suggested structure:

- Guide A — Soil peppers
- Guide B — Bato Bucket build (hydroponic peppers, specific system)
- Guide C — Hydroponic peppers (general)
- Guide D — General hydroponic primer (all crops)
- Guide E — Modern Masterblend / LED / precision
- Guide F — DIY nutrients (Masterblend from local materials)
- Guide G — DIY pH down (and pH up)
- Guide H — Grapes / viticulture

**4.4 "Growing Food" title on Guide D is misleading** relative to its actual scope (hydroponic primer). Rename, or move the calendar + crop-selection content into a cleaner document. Renaming is the lighter-weight fix.

**4.5 Guide C duplicates ~40% of Guide A.**

Decide whether Guide C is (a) a standalone hydroponic pepper guide that can be read without Guide A, or (b) a companion guide that assumes Guide A has been read. Recommendation: make it explicitly (b) — it reduces duplicate maintenance and strengthens the whole library.

**4.6 Closing paragraphs.**

Anchors A and H end with strong "sense of place" paragraphs. B, F, G do not. Adding them takes 10 minutes per guide and materially improves tonal alignment.
